## About Me

Artificial Intelligence attracted me because it represents one of the most powerful ideas humanity has ever pursued: the possibility of building systems that can learn, reason, adapt, and help people solve problems beyond the limits of individual human knowledge.

What inspires me most is not simply using AI to automate tasks. I am fascinated by understanding how intelligence itself can emerge. Questions such as how machines learn, how memory influences decisions, how reasoning develops, and how multiple cognitive systems cooperate continue to shape the way I think about engineering.

Rather than building conventional software, I enjoy creating systems that make intelligence visible. Through projects such as Mini FlyWire and Noetica, I explore how memory, planning, reasoning, reinforcement learning, and cognitive architectures can work together to produce adaptive behavior. For me, engineering is a way to study intelligence—not just implement algorithms.

My long-term vision extends beyond technology itself. I believe AI should become a trusted companion that helps people learn, think, create, and solve meaningful problems. Whether supporting students through personalized education, assisting researchers in scientific discovery, or helping individuals overcome loneliness, I want the systems I build to strengthen human capability rather than replace it.

Science fiction first inspired my curiosity, but engineering transformed that curiosity into purpose. Every project I build is an experiment in understanding how intelligent systems can become more useful, more understandable, and more aligned with human needs.

I am particularly interested in cognitive architectures, memory systems, AI agents, reasoning, reinforcement learning, and the engineering principles that make intelligent systems reliable, explainable, and continuously improving.

Ultimately, I do not see Artificial Intelligence as the destination.

I see it as humanity's most powerful tool for understanding ourselves, expanding our knowledge, and solving problems that today's world cannot yet solve.
